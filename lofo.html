LOFO

linking people with food

Lofo is an application that provides travelers with information of local food and restaurants. Travelers can also build connections with locals by going out and having a meal together. This is my very first design project.

0.

motivation

We, as human beings, all eat. Throughout history, the food that we eat had shaped civilizations and cultures. Thus, when traveling, eating local food can make the travelers' experiences better. So how would travelers, as strangers to a place, find places to eat?

1.

research

I did competitive analysis with 23andMe, which is a company that offers similar DNA testing services as Helix. Two conclusions were drawn.

  1. Helix lacks testings that are related to genetic diseases.
  2.  
  3. 23andMe's package includes testing for more than five genetic diseases, where Helix only has two single packages testing for inherited diabetes and familial hypercholesterolemia, each costing more than 23andMe's entire package.
  4.  
  5. Both services don't have testing for precision medicine information.
  1.  
  2. Precision medicine is the idea of personalized drug prescription based on one's omics such as genomics, which is a hot topic in the medical field currently. However, neither of the companies have such kind of service.

The Helix website showed that it did have an ongoing project on pharmacogenomics with Admera Health. Thus, an opportunity area would be adding pharmacogenomics testing to Helix's services.

 

However, when looking at the sample report presented by Admera Health, there were a lot of problems within. Further interviews with people also confirmed this, with them complaining about the unnecessary complexity and confusion when looking at the report.

2.

problem

Current pharmacogenomics data are not presented to the customers in a clear and understandable way. The reports customers get have three main issues:

 

  1. They have too much information, many of which is unnecessary.
  2.  
  3. They cause confusion to customers without medical knowledge.
  4.  
  5. They can't be updated easily according to the customers' needs after testing.

3.

solution

The solution is an application that kind of acts as an electronic medical record, which makes it easy for users to check their genomic data and understand the clinical interpretation according to their own needs.

report in hand

The user's report is easily synced to the user’s phone, making it accessible anywhere and anytime, without the worry of losing the paper report.

 

searching for drug

Users can easily search for drugs for the information relating to their genetic data.

easy interpretation

Clinical interpretations are written in an understandable way to the users. Color coding is used, with information that is most important prioritized.

adding dosage

Users can easily add the drugs that they are taking.

personalized drug list

The drug list is dynamic, so that the users can modify the list anytime they want, making the genomic data useful overtime.

Following the problem-solution model, Angela and I started identifying problems that contemporary travelers face when finding places to eat, by brainstorming on our own and through surveys and interviews with other people.

 

We came up with these general problems:

  • Gap between local residents and travelers
  • Lack of trustworthy restaurant finder
  • Lack of knowledge of local dishes
  • People's different needs from the app
  • Travelers want to try real home food

With the problems identified, Angela and I started the initial ideation of the app.

 

A stakeholder map was created by Angela, identifying target users of the app.
