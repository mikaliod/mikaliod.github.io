Helix Redesign

rethinking the value of dna testing

0.

motivation

When choosing the company to redesign for for the KPCB design challenge, Helix caught my attention at first sight. It is a personal DNA testing service, which seemed like a really good place to look into as it is at the intersection of science and design. With the increased medical significance and popularity of genetic testing, the potential of such services would be very promising.

1.

research

I did competitive analysis with 23andMe, which is a company that offers similar DNA testing services as Helix.

 

One conclusion is that Helix lacks testings that are related to genetic diseases. 23andMe's package includes testing for 5+ genetic diseases, where Helix only has two single packages testing for inherited diabetes and familial hypercholesterolemia, each costing more than 23andMe's entire package. Another conclusion is that both services don't have testing for precision medicine information. Precision medicine is the idea of personalized drug prescription based on one's omics such as genomics, which is a hot topic in the medical field currently.

 

The Helix website show that it does have a project on pharmacogenomics with Admera Health. Thus, an opportunity area would be adding pharmacogenomics testing to Helix's services. However, when looking at the sample report presented by Admera Health, there are a lot of problems within. Further interviews with people also confirms this, with them complaining about the unnecessary complexity and confusion when looking at the report.

2.

problem

Current pharmacogenomics data are not presented to the customers in a clear and understandable way. The reports that customers get have way too much information, many of which are unnecessary, causes confusion to customers without medical knowledge, and can't be updated easily after a period of time after the genetic testing according to the customers' needs.

3.

solution

The solution is an application that acts as an electronic medical record, which makes it easy for users to check their genomic data and understand the clinical interpretation according to their own needs.

The user's report is easily synced to the user’s phone, making it accessible anywhere and anytime, without the worry of losing the paper report.

 

Users can easily search for drugs for the information relating to their genetic data.

Clinical interpretations are written in an understandable way to the users. Color coding is used, with information that is most important prioritized.

Users can easily add the drugs that they are taking.

The drug list is dynamic, so that the users can modify the list anytime they want, making the genomic data useful overtime.
