How might we facilitate patient-centric empathy?

Challenge

Outcome

Healthcare systems today are facing new challenges as well as new opportunities with newer forms of technology such as telehealth. Despite these, the relationship between patients and their medical providers remains vital, where a lack of empathy could lead to bad patient experience or, even worse, poor medical outcome.

In 5 months, our team conducted research with patients and medical providers, synthesized insights, and designed Patiently, a healthcare portal application, with the focus on features that encourage patients to share their thoughts, feelings, and stories with their providers.

 

Role

Teammates

Our team worked together on conducting research, synthesizing findings, ideation, prototyping, and user testing. Individually, I led the probe part of research, and was responsible for the visual, interaction, and animation design of this app iteration shown on this page.

Jamaal Baki, Jenny Chu

Design Overview

Patiently is a healthcare portal application that encourage patients to share their thoughts, feelings, and stories with their providers.

 

Starting check in

Patients can check in for their upcoming appointments in locations where they feel relaxed and safe.

Onboarding

The onboarding process aims to motivate patients to share their thoughts and feelings.

Choosing who to share

Patients have the power to select who to share their stories with, according to their own comfort level.

Selecting a prompt

The various prompts provide patients with the freedom to choose and also serve as inspirations for them to think and reflect.

Recording an answer

Patients can use the most humane form of communication - voice to answer prompts, with typing text as an alternative.

Choosing top prompts

Patients can choose up to 3 top prompts that they think are the most important. This also benefits providers, as they can prioritize these starred prompts if time is constrained.
