How could future mobility help bridge communities?

Challenge

Outcome

Pittsburgh is growing at a fast pace with the increase number in students and young working professionals coming into the city. This influx of individuals, however, is creating a social division between the old and the new, causing issues such as gentrification in many of Pittsburgh’s neighborhoods. Our team worked with Bridgestone to ideate how this problem can be solved through future technology in mobility.

Our team designed a vehicle with adjustable and expandable walls. These vehicles can form assemblies and create event spaces in city neighborhoods that facilitate social interactions between communities.

People research

We started the design process by doing a stakeholder analysis, which allowed us to target key areas for further research. We identified that

We reached to conclusion that, when it comes to finances, though a lot of influence on the parents and teens are similar, Banks' influence is only on the parents. This led us to move research direction towards exploring the parent-teen relationship in terms of finance.

Stakeholder interviews

Literature reviews

We interviewed representatives from PNC Bank about their established effort in financial literacy, the resources they have to offer, and their vision of what can be promising directions.

We reviewed 10 papers whose topics range from the current state of financial literacy among teenagers to evaluation of financial education programs / mobile applications.

Competitive analysis

Online surveys

We analyzed 7 other products on the market whose purposes are to improve financial literacy among teenagers and students, including CMU Student Financial Services, Bank of America, Capital One, ThreeJars, etc.

We set up an online survey to young students in college asking them about their financial behavior, and another survey to parents inquiring about their involvement in their children's financial education.

These generative researches led to several key findings:

Finance is seen as a personal issue, but it can be a community/family topic.

Managing finances is usually initiated by a trigger.

Programs promoting financial literacy should be tailored to an individual’s knowledge, ability, demographics, life stage, and learning styles.

Most successful applications maintain engagement with people by giving continuous, immediate, and unambiguous feedback.

Problem Framing

Problem statement

Opportunity area

Though PNC want to engage with these teens by helping to promote their financial literacy, it does not have a direct influence to teens.

PNC can leverage the parent-teen relationship and provide a financial service to the teens and the whole family, which would be personalized and provide continuous trigger.

Ideation

Starting off from the opportunity area, we began ideating how to solve the problem.

Around these personae, we wrote scenarios and brainstormed how we can solve these personae's frustrations. In the end, we narrowed down to 5 possible solutions, created storyboards for each, and tested these ideas with people by speed-dating.

5 / 5

From the speed-dating sessions, we received the most positive reactions for the 30-day challenge  idea, where an application would present financial challenges for families to complete. Feedbacks from the users include:

  1. They wanted to make the time period for challenges shorter.
  2. Teenagers wanted greater incentives to invest time into the challenges.

We improved the idea by reducing the challenges timespan, and creating a reward system that would drive engagement. We then began designing the application based upon this idea.

Solution

The solution is Financial Feud, an application that provides families with weekly financial challenges.

Each week a family focus on a specific financial domain, and the application assigns each family member tasks to complete. The challenges are designed to create accountability and competitive spirit between family members.

These challenges encourage teenagers to build basic financial skills while building a relationship with PNC Bank.

Future Steps

In the future we would like to work more on the visual design of the application as well as the design of the challenges themselves. We see this service being used to build community financial literacy as well in the future. With the spread of the service, group challenges with friends or individual challenges can also be implemented in the system. This would help in building the credibility of PNC Bank with more communities  engaging in PNC's services.
