Financial Feud

a bank service promoting financial literacy

How can banks help increase teens' financial literacy? PNC Bank reached out to us in search of a service design to answer that question. The solution by our team is an application that provides families with weekly financial challenges, which encourages teens to build basic financial skills while building a relationship with PNC. This is a class project for Service Design.

1.

domain research

To understand the domain better, research about the PNC Financial Literacy Program, competitive analysis, and general survey were done. The researches led to several key findings:

  • Managing finances is usually initiated by a trigger.
  •  
  • Finance is seen as a personal issue, but it can be a community/family topic.
  •  
  • Literacy is closely related to sociodemographic and family financial sophistication.
  •  
  • Financial literacy should be tailored to an individual’s knowledge, ability, demographics, life stage, and learning styles.
  •  
  • We need to stop trying to change people and start harnessing their biases to work in favor of good financial decisions.
  •  
  • Most successful applications maintain engagement with people by giving continuous, immediate, and unambiguous feedback.

2.

stakeholder analysis

A general stakeholder map was first drawn.

During the analysis, several points were drawn.

  • Parents have a strong and direct influence on teens.
  •  
  • The influence on the parents and teens are similar.
  •  
  • Bank has a relationship with only the parents.

This lead us to redesign the stakeholder map in a way that showed the above points clearly, and we decided to target the parents and then break to the teens through them in a way that can benefit the whole family.

3.

problem statement and opportunity area

The problem is defined to be that PNC Bank does not have a direct link of influence to teens between the ages of 12 and 21. They want to engage these teens by increasing their financial literacy.

 

The opportunity area identified is that PNC can leverage the parent/teen relationship to provide a financial service to teens and the overall family.

4.

ideation

Based on the problem and opportunity area we identified, we started ideating how to solve the problem.

Persona and scenarios were made, each targeting a group in a family (i.e teens who are in high school, teens who are in the first year of college, parents). You can find detailed info from page 18 in the process book.

Based on the persona and scenarios, we then brainstormed and narrowed down the possible solution down to five. Storyboards were created for each, and the value added to PNC Bank was also analyzed. You can find detailed info from page 23 in the process book. We then tested these ideas with people by speed dating.

From the speed dating, we received the most positive reactions for the 30 day challenge application, which presents financial challenges for families to complete. However, users wanted to modify the challenges to make the time period shorter for each. We also found that teens wanted greater incentives to invest time into the challenges. This leaded us to improve the idea by reducing the challenges timespan and creating rewards that drive engagement. We then began designing the application based upon this idea.

5.

solution

The solution is Financial Feud, an application that provides families with weekly financial challenges.

 

Every week the application focus on a different financial domain and assign each family member a task to complete. The challenges are designed to create accountability and competitive spirit between family members.

 

These tasks encourage teens to build basic financial skills while building a relationship with PNC Bank.

6.

future steps

In the future we see this application being used to build community financial literacy as well. This can be shared by teens with their friends and they can have group challenges. Similar concepts can be applied to the parents too.

This also helps in building the credibility of PNC Bank where more communities will vouch for them and engage in their services.
